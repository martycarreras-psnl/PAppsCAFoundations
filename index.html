The Real Question

Why Not Just Deploy to Azure Websites with SQL?

It's a fair question. Azure is powerful. SQL is proven. But when you choose that path, you're choosing to build and maintain capabilities that already exist — tested, secured, and governed — inside the Power Platform.

What you build yourself

  • Row-level security — hand-roll authorization middleware, per-table policies, audit who accessed what
  • Business rules — write validation logic, wire up server-side triggers, maintain edge cases forever
  • Audit logging — instrument every create, update, delete; build retention policies; satisfy compliance reviews
  • Calculated & rollup fields — add SQL computed columns, maintain materialized views, debug aggregation drift
  • Schema migration — write migration scripts, version them, test rollbacks, handle tenant branching
  • API surface — design REST endpoints, implement OData or GraphQL, document contracts
  • Mobile readiness — adopt a cross-platform framework or build native wrappers
  • Accessibility compliance — implement WCAG 2.1 AA across every custom component
  • Environment ALM — build CI/CD for schema, data seeding, configuration, and secrets per environment

What Dataverse gives you on day one

  • Row-level security — configurable per table, per business unit, per team, per user. Zero custom code
  • Business rules — declarative, versioned, environment-aware. Applied at the data layer, not the UI
  • Full audit history — every record change tracked, with actor, timestamp, and field-level diffs built in
  • Calculated & rollup fields — defined in metadata, computed by the platform, always consistent
  • Managed solutions — schema, data, security roles, connection refs packaged and deployed as a unit
  • Web API — standard OData v4 REST surface auto-generated from your schema, with $filter, $expand, $orderby
  • Power Apps mobile — your Code App runs in the native Power Apps mobile shell out of the box
  • Accessibility — Fluent UI components are WCAG 2.1 AA compliant; the shell handles focus management
  • Environment ALM — dev → test → prod promotion via managed solutions, with connection mapping
Understanding all options expands the boundaries of creativity — in an age where creativity is the human differentiator.

The Frontier

Copilot Studio & Agentic Automation

Your Code App isn't just a UI. It's a surface for intelligent agents that reason, take action, and integrate with any MCP-enabled service — all within your organization's governance boundary.

🤖

Autonomous Agents

Copilot Studio agents can execute multi-step workflows triggered by your app — approvals, escalations, data enrichment — without human intervention between steps.

🧠

MCP Server Access

Agents can now reach Model Context Protocol servers, giving them real-time access to external tools, databases, and APIs that extend beyond the connector ecosystem.

🛡️

Governed by Design

Every agent action respects your tenant's DLP policies, Dataverse security roles, and audit trail. Enterprise-grade AI automation without shadow IT risk.

💡

Assistive In-App Copilot

Embed a Copilot Studio agent directly in your Code App for contextual assistance — answering questions, surfacing related records, or guiding users through complex forms.

Intelligence Built In

Work IQ: Your App Learns

WorkIQ is the intelligence layer that personalizes Copilot to you and your organization. When your app runs on Dataverse, WorkIQ can observe, learn, and optimize — turning your application into a living system that gets smarter over time.

🛠️

Skills & Tools

Provides specialized instructions to customize Copilot and agents to deliver tailored experiences optimized for specific tasks within your application's domain.

🔄

Context

Uses memory to learn from your unique style, preferences, habits, and workflows — understanding your patterns over time and personalizing every interaction.

📊

Data

Combines everyday work with external data, including M365 signals and line-of-business data, to give Copilot real-time understanding of your organization's context.

Why this matters for enterprise teams: An Azure website with SQL is a black box — you know it runs, but you don't know how people use it. A Dataverse app integrated with WorkIQ is observable, optimizable, and governable. Process mining automatically discovers how users actually flow through your app versus how you designed it. Task mining surfaces repetitive manual steps that should be automated. And leadership gets clear visibility into where automation investment delivers the highest ROI.

This isn't a feature you bolt on later. It's an inherent capability of the platform — available the moment your app stores its first record in Dataverse.

What Makes This Different

AI That Plans Before It Codes

Most templates hand you scaffolding. This one teaches AI to interrogate your business problem, decompose it into actionable dimensions, and refine the solution boundary — before a single line of code is written.

Articulate

Describe your business problem in your own words — messy, incomplete, full of assumptions. The instruction files guide Copilot to interpret your narrative, separate explicit facts from implied assumptions, and identify what's missing.

Decompose & Refine

AI decomposes your narrative across 12 business dimensions — workflows, approvals, automation, Teams integration, reporting, governance. It challenges weak assumptions and asks only the highest-value follow-up questions.

Architect & Build

Only when the scope is stable does the system translate into Dataverse entities, relationships, lifecycle states, and security patterns — then into the schema planning artifacts that drive the rest of the template.
